Andrew's Flic List

My Top Favorite Movies

How To Train Your Dragon

The first movie of the trilogy was also the best one as far as I'm concerned. Some mistery and suspense, great action, and a cast full of lovable and unique characters make this piece a must see regardless of age. Based on a real book, its plot stands out among other animated films, delivering a memorable fun time for the whole family.

Falling Down

This older movie is still incredible. Chills will run down your spine, as you watch the protagonist (a seemingly decent man) one day just snap under the pressure of his pathetic life in the city. In the course of the film, we are then faced with the ugliness, the filth, and the failure of modern society, as our hero tries to make his way back home to his estranged family. We are treated to witness how the so called American Dream really works out for the losers (and the winners) in their patented Rat Race. But in the end, too late, our hero discovers his own shame as well. That he always lived as he was told; only to be betrayed as they all lied to him.

Interview With The Vampire

This is the only vamp film that I know of, that bothered to try conveying more than a cheap thrill of blood and suck. Therefore it gets my respect, but more importantly I still remember its key points and its ambience decades later.
